Our Data

Our dataset is comprised of over 5,000 clinical records containing data for 8 different indicators of heart stroke. These indicators span demographic and medical data, from age and gender to average glucose level and history of heart disease. We created several graphs to provide quick visual access to the data, shown below. By clicking through the graphs, you'll see stroke distribution for each of our key indicators. While some distributions are comparatively even, others, such as stroke distribution by history of heart disease are clearly unbalanced.


We know that not all indicators are created equal, though. We looked at the importance each indicator has when running our model to better understand how the model is working and to provide insight into how risk of stroke can be identified and mitigated. Gender, age, and hypertension are the three indicators with the most importance; while it may be disheartening to find that the two leading indicators are factors outside of your control, it further highlights the importance of managing hypertension to maintain a low risk of stroke.
