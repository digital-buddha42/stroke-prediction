Heart Stroke Risk Calculator

Using machine learning, we can accurately predict your risk of stroke based on several key indicators. Our model compares your data against over 5000 records from real patients to determine your risk of stroke. Predicting stroke is an important medical advancement that can help reduce the burden on individuals and society. To be clear, this calculator does not constitute a medical diagnosis. Please consult your primary care physician before making any health decisions. However, our model does demonstrate how machine learning can be used to analyze medical and demographic data to provide valuable insights into our health. You can enter your information in our calculator to get your results. We've produced insightful visualizations about our dataset on our Visualizations page. For more information on our model and data, keep reading below.

Our Model

To make our calculator functional, we trained a Random Forest model to predict heart stroke risk. Our model evaluates gender, marriage history, employment history, residence type, smoking history, age, average glucose level, and BMI. With raw data, our model performed with 94% accuracy. For our purposes, the raw data is skewed heavily -- 95% of the records in the dataset contained data for individuals who did not have a stroke. To better train our model to recognize indicators of stroke, we used Smote to create more records for individuals who had had a stroke. After using Smote, our model performs at 89% accuracy.

The Data

Our data comes from Kaggle.com. Over 5,000 records of 11 clinical features for predicting stroke events were compiled. Distribution of stroke in the records skewed heavily to 'no stroke' (95% of the dataset). This data comes from a confidential source. If used in research, please credit the author.
